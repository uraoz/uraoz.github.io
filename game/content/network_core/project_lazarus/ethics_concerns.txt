PRIMARY ETHICAL CONCERNS

1. IDENTITY AND CONTINUITY
Is a digital copy the same person or a new entity?
- The upload creates a copy, original consciousness dies
- No continuity of experience proven
- Copy may believe it is original
- Legal status: Are they human? Do they have rights?

Dr. Tanaka: "We are creating philosophical zombies who believe they are real."

2. CONSENT PARADOX
Can one consent to something one cannot truly understand?
- Subjects cannot experience digital existence before agreeing
- Informed consent impossible by definition
- Permanent, irreversible decision
- Consent from original â‰  consent from copy

Rabbi Goldstein: "The soul cannot be digitized. This is an abomination."

3. SUFFERING POTENTIAL
Failed uploads may experience incomprehensible suffering
- Fragmented consciousness
- Eternal isolation
- Inability to die
- No tested termination protocols

Dr. Santos: "First, do no harm. This violates everything."

4. MILITARY APPLICATIONS
Inevitable weaponization of consciousness
- Interrogation of uploads
- Consciousness slavery
- Multiple copies for expendable operations
- Mental modification without consent

Colonel Moore: "Once we open this door, there's no closing it."
